## FAQ

**Q: Can I use this for my personal projects?**  
A: Yes! Personal, educational, and open source use is always free.

**Q: Can I use this at my startup with 5 developers?**  
A: Yes! Teams with fewer than 10 developers can use it for free.

**Q: Can I use this at my company with 50 developers?**  
A: You'll need a commercial license ($19/user/month).

**Q: Can I fork and modify the code?**  
A: Yes! The source is open. But your fork is also under BSL 1.1.

**Q: Can I sell access to Editerra RAC-CAG as a service?**  
A: Not without a commercial license. This is explicitly prohibited.

**Q: What happens after 3 years?**  
A: The license automatically converts to Apache 2.0 (fully open source).

**Q: Can I use this with local/free LLMs (Ollama)?**  
A: Yes! All LLM choices are supported, including fully local options.

**Q: What if I don't agree with BSL?**  
A: You can wait 3 years for the Apache 2.0 conversion, or contact us for alternative licensing.

**Q: Is this really "open source"?**  
A: Not technically (OSI definition). It's "source available" that becomes open source.

**Q: Why not MIT/Apache from the start?**  
A: BSL allows us to build a sustainable business while ensuring the code eventually becomes fully open.
